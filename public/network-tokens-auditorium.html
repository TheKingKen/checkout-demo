What is a network token?

A network token is a card-network-issued stand-in for the real card number. The real card details stay hidden behind the scenes while payments run using this safer digital identity.

  • Issued by Visa or Mastercard networks.
  • Unique to your merchant context.
  • Works with one-time transaction cryptograms.

Why people care

Customers keep paying smoothly even when their physical card expires or is replaced. Businesses see fewer failed recurring charges and less checkout friction.

  • Fewer declines from expired cards.
  • Better fraud resistance due to cryptograms.
  • Potential uplift in authorization rates.

Simple analogy

Merchant-stored card is like saving a contact in your phone. Network token is like that contact being maintained by the mobile network itself, so the number stays reachable even if the SIM card changes.

How provisioning works in 4 easy steps

1

Card entered once

The customer enters card details during a normal checkout.

2

Provisioning requested

Checkout.com asks Visa or Mastercard for a network token linked to that card.

3

Token issued and stored

The network token is saved in Vault and tied to future payments.

4

Future payments improve

Next charges use token + cryptogram, while lifecycle updates are handled automatically.

When you would call provisioning APIs directly

  • You are migrating a large existing card base and want token readiness before the next billing run.
  • You have your own card-on-file vault and need to tokenize those cards through Checkout.com.
  • You need synchronous behavior for specific first-transaction policies.
  • You want to export network tokens and request cryptograms for use with another processor.
